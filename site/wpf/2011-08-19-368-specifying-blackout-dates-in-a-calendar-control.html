#368 – Specifying Blackout Dates in a Calendar Control

2011-08-19 | Original URL | Wayback 20120425222552

August 19, 2011 1 Comment

You can use the BlackoutDates property of a Calendar control to specify dates that a user cannot select.

The BlackoutDates property is of type CalendarBlackoutDatesCollection, which is an observable collection of CalendarDateRange objects.  You can specify a set of blackout dates for a Calendar control in XAML by defining a series of CalendarDateRange instances.

<Calendar Name="calMuseum" SelectionMode="SingleDate">
    <Calendar.BlackoutDates>
        <CalendarDateRange Start="8/6/11" End="8/7/11"/>
        <CalendarDateRange Start="8/20/11" End="8/20/11"/>
        <CalendarDateRange Start="8/28/11" End="8/30/11"/>
    </Calendar.BlackoutDates>
</Calendar>
<Label Content="Choose a day to visit the museum." HorizontalContentAlignment="Center" />

The dates specified as blackout dates are not selectable by a user and will appear x‘d out on the calendar.

You can also add specific blackout dates from code-behind.

// Blackout holidays
calMuseum.BlackoutDates.Add(new CalendarDateRange(new DateTime(class="num">2011,class="num">1,class="num">17)));
calMuseum.BlackoutDates.Add(new CalendarDateRange(new DateTime(class="num">2011,class="num">2,class="num">21)));
calMuseum.BlackoutDates.Add(new CalendarDateRange(new DateTime(class="num">2011,class="num">5,class="num">30)));
calMuseum.BlackoutDates.Add(new CalendarDateRange(new DateTime(class="num">2011,class="num">7,class="num">4)));
calMuseum.BlackoutDates.Add(new CalendarDateRange(new DateTime(class="num">2011,class="num">9,class="num">5)));
calMuseum.BlackoutDates.Add(new CalendarDateRange(new DateTime(class="num">2011,class="num">10,class="num">10)));
calMuseum.BlackoutDates.Add(new CalendarDateRange(new DateTime(class="num">2011,class="num">11,class="num">11)));
calMuseum.BlackoutDates.Add(new CalendarDateRange(new DateTime(class="num">2011,class="num">11,class="num">24)));
calMuseum.BlackoutDates.Add(new CalendarDateRange(new DateTime(class="num">2011,class="num">12,class="num">26)));
